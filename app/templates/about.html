About the Developer

Frances Duffy is a PhD Candidate at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. She studies U.S. intervention in foreign conflict and the effects of local level civilian-militant interactions on overall foreign policy outcomes. Her dissertation examines how varying forms of U.S. intervention can alter the incentives of conflict actors, with a focus on Afghanistan, Somalia, Libya and the Sahel. Mrs. Duffy previously served for five years as a Foreign Service Officer with the U.S. Department of State, working in Belgium, Tunisia, and South Africa with additional travel to nineteen U.S. missions throughout Africa. She holds a Bachelor of Science degree in Mechanical Engineering from Columbia University.
